Ella
Ella is a small town nestled in Sri Lanka’s central highlands, famous for its breathtaking views, lush greenery, and cool climate. It’s a popular destination for hiking, with highlights like Ella Rock, Little Adam’s Peak, and the iconic Nine Arches Bridge.

Sigiriya
Sigiriya, also known as the Lion Rock, is an ancient rock fortress in central Sri Lanka. Rising 200 meters above the jungle, it features stunning frescoes, landscaped gardens, and a royal palace built by King Kashyapa in the 5th century. A UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Yala is Sri Lanka’s most famous national park, known for its rich wildlife and beautiful landscapes. Home to elephants, leopards, sloth bears, and hundreds of bird species, Yala offers thrilling safari experiences.

Arugambay
Arugam Bay is a laid-back beach town on Sri Lanka’s east coast, famous for its world-class surfing waves and relaxed vibe. Surrounded by palm-fringed beaches and scenic lagoons, it’s a hotspot for surfers, backpackers, and nature lovers. Beyond the waves, Arugam Bay offers stunning sunrises, and vibrant local culture.

Galle
Galle is a historic coastal city in southern Sri Lanka, best known for the Galle Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site built by the Portuguese and fortified by the Dutch. With its cobbled streets, colonial architecture, boutique shops, and ocean views, Galle offers a charming blend of history, culture, and seaside beauty.

Wilpattuwa
Wilpattu is Sri Lanka’s largest national park, famous for its natural lakes called "willus." It's a peaceful wildlife haven, home to leopards, elephants, sloth bears, and many bird species.
